TCP Congestion Control Notes
============================

1. Slow Start:
- TCP begins with a small congestion window (cwnd).
- cwnd grows exponentially, usually doubling each RTT, until packet loss or ssthresh is reached.

2. Congestion Avoidance:
- After slow start threshold (ssthresh), cwnd grows linearly.
- Additive increase prevents aggressive bursts.

3. Loss Detection:
- Timeout indicates severe congestion; cwnd resets to 1 MSS and ssthresh is reduced.
- Triple duplicate ACK indicates moderate congestion.

4. Fast Retransmit:
- On three duplicate ACKs, sender retransmits lost packet immediately.

5. Fast Recovery:
- Instead of returning to full slow start, sender reduces cwnd and recovers faster.
- Typical behavior: set ssthresh = cwnd/2, then continue with congestion avoidance.

Practical takeaway:
TCP aims for fairness and stability by increasing slowly when network is near capacity and reducing quickly after loss.
